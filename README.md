# Business Model Deep-Dive
## Revenue Streams:

Nestlé operates a highly diversified portfolio, minimizing reliance on any single market or product category. As of the latest 2024/2025 data, revenue is generated through seven primary product categories and five geographic zones.

### Product Category Breakdown

- **Powdered & Liquid Beverages (Key Driver):** Remains the largest contributor (~27% of sales), driven by iconic brands like Nescafé, Nespresso, and Starbucks.

- **PetCare (High Growth):** The second-largest and fastest-growing engine (Purina), capitalizing on the "pet humanization" trend.

- **Nutrition & Health Science:** Includes infant nutrition (NAN, Gerber) and medical nutrition, offering high margins and resilience.

- **Others:** Prepared Dishes, Milk Products, Confectionery (KitKat), and Water.

##  Valuation Methodology

The target price was derived using a **5-Year Discounted Cash Flow (DCF)** model under a **Base Case** scenario.

### 1. Key Assumptions (Inputs)
*   **WACC (Weighted Average Cost of Capital):** 4.52%
    *   *Risk-Free Rate (Rf):* 2.56% (Based on 10Y Govt Bond Yield)
    *   *Beta:* 0.52
    *   *Equity Risk Premium (MRP):* 4.33%
    *   *Cost of Debt (Rd):* 4.25%
*   **Terminal Growth Rate (TGR):** 2%
*   **Tax Rate:** ~20%

### 2. DCF Model Output (Base Case)
*   **Enterprise Value (EV):** CHF 280.85 Billion
*   **Equity Value:** CHF 233.96 Billion
*   **Shares Outstanding:** 2.62 Billion
*   **Implied Share Price:** **CHF 89.30**

## Risks
*   **Input Cost Inflation:** Rising prices of raw materials (cocoa, coffee, sugar) could pressure gross margins.
*   **Currency Fluctuation:** Significant exposure to foreign exchange risks as a global multinational reporting in CHF.
*   **Changing Consumer Habits:** Shift towards private labels or healthier alternatives could impact market share.
